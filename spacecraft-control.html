Spacecraft Attitude Dynamics and Control

Nonlinear quaternion feedback control of a reaction-wheel spacecraft (PROBA-2 scenario) in MATLAB/Simulink: derived RW-augmented dynamics, built guidance (GDC) + control (CTL) blocks, added two-body orbit propagation and environmental torques (drag, SRP, gravity-gradient, magnetic), and tuned gains via the alpha–beta method.

Key Details

  • Methods:
    Implemented total angular momentum + wheel dynamics and Euler’s equation with three reaction wheels; quaternion kinematics and CBI conversion; closed-loop quaternion feedback with qd = [0, 0, 0, 1]ᵀ; four Kp selection strategies (Scaled, Inverse-Inertia, Eigenaxis); Kd from ωₙ = 0.156 rad/s and ~50 s settling-time spec
  • Tools:
    MATLAB/Simulink subsystems: DYN, ACT (RW), GDC, CTL, ORB, TEX, and Star Tracker (QUA2CBI).
  • Outcome:
    Produced wheel-torque inputs; spacecraft quaternions and angular-rate histories; qi–qj phase plots for all four gain-selection methods; orbital distance/speed over one revolution; net perturbing torque vs time; and closed-loop runs showing convergence under disturbances after alpha–beta tuning.
