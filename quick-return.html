Crank, Link, Repeat - Quick-Return Dynamics

A six-link, single-DoF quick-return mechanism analyzed across multiple poses to quantify velocities, accelerations, joint forces, and input torque, then validated with a 3D Simscape Multibody simulation.

Key Details

  • Methods:
    • Worked the AERO 3002 flow: market & mission pitch → initial sizing/constraint analysis → configuration down-select → aero/performance/loads → cost & compliance; documented in a proposal and a final design report per course deliverables.
    • Selected a 6-link, 1-DoF quick-return mechanism; verified mobility with the Kutzbach criterion (m = 1).
    • Performed kinematic analysis via velocity and acceleration polygons for four states (including Coriolis terms), extracting ω and α for critical links.
    • Conducted force analysis with FBDs; formulated and solved linear systems for unknown joint reactions and required input torque at each pose.
    • Compared states to study the effect of reducing the rise angle of link CD on link speeds and accelerations.
    • Built a Simscape Multibody model to visualize motion and corroborate hand-calc trends.
  • Tools:
    • Modeling/Simulation: MATLAB & Simulink (Simscape Multibody).
    • Analysis: Classical planar mechanisms (velocity/acceleration polygons), matrix solution for statics/dynamics, tabulated summaries.
  • Outcome:
    • Confirmed m = 1 mobility and quantified link behavior across four states (e.g., ω₃ and α₂/α₃ trends), with clear evidence that lowering the rise angle of link CD increases most tangential/Coriolis components.
    • Computed pose-wise input torque and major joint forces, producing consolidated tables for velocities, accelerations, angular accelerations, and forces.
    • Delivered a working 3D simulation and a rigorous report that future teams can reuse for optimization or controller design.
