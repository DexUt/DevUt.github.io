Egg Mover Project

Our team designed a free-standing system to lift, transport, and precisely place a raw chicken egg from one 4×4 ft square to another 4 ft away, clearing a 3-in barrier. The concept uses a lightweight cart running on overhead tracks with a motorized claw: one DC motor opens/closes the claw, a second raises/lowers it, and a third translates the cart via a nylon rope and pulley run. A Raspberry Pi provides motor control from a 12 V DC supply. The design includes a functional 3D-printed claw and delivers a complete drawing package (3-view, assembly, and working drawings) to enable rapid build and test.

Key Details

  • Methods:
    • Interpreted task and constraints: floor-supported system; start inside the source square ≥12 in above the plane; wireless/electrical operation only; include ≥1 functional 3D-printed part (≤3 in³ material); egg must arrive intact within 30 s.
    • Selected a claw-on-cart architecture: claw picks up the egg, lifts to clear the wall, then the cart traverses to the target square; three discrete mechanical motions satisfy the simplicity constraint.
    • Performed sizing checks (mass/torque/friction) to verify motors can raise the claw/egg and translate the cart; identified the long rope run as a potential failure point (slack/tangle risk).
    • Proposed iteration: mount the translation motor on the cart to directly drive an axle via gearing, reducing rope complexity
  • Tools:
    • Hardware: Wood foundation (2×4/2×6 and ½-in plywood) with two elevated track rails; cart with plastic/rubber wheels on coat-hanger axles; three DC motors; nylon rope + pulleys; metal gears; T-plates; 12 V supply.
    • 3D-printed part: PETG (report lists “PTEG”) split-bucket claw optimized for egg geometry (~2.7 in³).
    • Electronics/Control: Raspberry Pi for motor control; 22-gauge wiring; wireless/electrical operation per brief.
    • Deliverables: Full drawing set—3-view layout, assembly, and working drawings—for fabrication.
  • Outcome:
    • Finalized a realistic, repeatable design under a $150 target budget, aimed to place the egg within 2 in of the bullseye in <30 s (team goal: <20 s).
    • Validated feasibility analytically (torque, mass, friction) and documented risks with concrete improvements (direct-drive cart, lighter foundation).
    • Produced a build-ready package enabling future prototyping and testing.
