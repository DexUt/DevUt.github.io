Weather Satellite Project

Completed for AERO 3841 Spacecraft Design I (Pre-Phase-A study), this concept designs a weather-monitoring satellite to collect imagery plus atmospheric data (temperature, pressure, humidity, wind) and downlink to the Gatineau Ground Station. We selected a sun-synchronous LEO and sized the bus, power, comms, ADCS, thermal, propulsion, and launcher. The payload (200 kg, 350 W, 1.5 m Ø × 1 m) drives a cylindrical bus sized to ~3.03 m³. The design achieves daily access ~80 min, total data rate ~4.97×10⁵ bps, solar array ~12.27 m² with ~2.6 kWh battery, and ~73.6 kg propellant for station-keeping and controlled deorbit.

Methods

  • Chose a sun-synchronous LEO for global, repeatable coverage; example orbital elements used: a≈7178 km, i≈98.6°, e≈0.00012; lifetime 5 yr, readiness 3 yr.
  • Built mass & power budgets from heritage data; dry mass with margin ~739.7 kg; total power with 10% margin ~793.8 W.
  • Comms sizing to Gatineau station (>10° elevation): MATLAB access analysis gave ~80 min/day; QPSK links at ~397 kbps down / ~99 kbps up with link margins 10.4 dB / 28.45 dB.
  • Power subsystem: worst eclipse ~35.1 min; regulated 25 V DET bus; Si arrays ~12.27 m² (EOL ~1.58 kW) and battery ~2.58 kWh; harness ~14 kg.
  • GNC/ADCS: three-axis, zero-momentum wheels + thrusters + mag torquers; sensors include coarse sun sensors, IR horizon, GPS, and 3-axis magnetometers; nadir-pointing with roll-yaw coupling.
  • Thermal: MLI-wrapped sphere-equivalent analysis; WCH satisfied (≈34.3 °C), WCC requires heaters (~4.82 kW) and TEC for payload 20 ± 1 °C → hybrid thermal approach.
  • Propulsion: bipropellant system sized for orbit control and deorbit (ΔV_deorbit ≈253 m/s); propellant ~73.6 kg including margins.
  • Launcher trade: PSLV vs Falcon 9; Falcon 9 selected via weighted trade (performance/availability) for SSO rideshare.

Tools

  • MATLAB (Satellite Toolbox) for access and geometry; spreadsheet sizing for budgets; literature & SMAD-based assumptions for links, power, and thermal.

Outcome

  • Produced a defensible Pre-Phase-A concept meeting the course brief with complete subsystem first-cuts, link and power closure, and a viable launcher path.
  • Identified next steps: refine SSO altitude/MLST trade, add detailed pointing/jitter analysis, iterate array/battery mass vs. heater load, and validate link margins with full atmospheric/rain models.
